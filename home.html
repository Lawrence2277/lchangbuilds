3D Printed Ebike Solution

Designed and fabricated by Lawrence Chang

June 2017-May 2018

The goal of this project was to facilitate a fast, effortless commute to school as soon possible and for as cheap as possible. In order to keep the costs down and to make the build as accessible as possible, all parts can be either 3d printed or bought off the shelf for this electric bike system.

The Build

The electric bike’s motion system was designed in Solidworks. The mount clamps around the seat tube and bolts into the water bottle holder inserts that come standard on most bike frames. A piece of inner tube is added between the plastic mount and metal tube for additional friction.

The conversion is meant for any generic single speed fixie bike with a flip flop hub. A 90 tooth #25 sprocket threaded onto the hub.

The conversion uses a 24v 350w brushed DC pancake motor. I am overvolting it at 36v and running on an 800w controller. A 36v, 8.8ah lithium ion battery can take this bike approximately 12 miles on a single charge with a top speed of 25mph*(only in perfect riding conditions).

Many many profiles and iterations were created before arriving at the present day model. Lots of waste plastic....good thing we also have a filament recycler(**wink wink nudge nudge...go check it out! :p)

Filament Recycler

Designed and fabricated by Lawrence Chang

Feburary 2017-May 2017

This is a filament making machine, used to melt plastic mulch into useable 3D printer filament.

I had recently purchased a 3D printer after saving up enough money from selling fidget spinners at my high school(this was before they became a meme :p). After accumulating many failed prints, I built this recycler to remelt the plastic mulch into useable filament to help negate the costs of new filament and reduce waste!

The Build

This project uses a generic PID temp controller module, SSR, and k-type thermocouple to regulate temperature of the hot end, which is composed of a steel pipe.

A geared, brushed DC motor drives, an auger bit, inside of the steel tube and pushes the plastic forward.

Cooling apparatus is composed of two 40mm DC fans with a polycarbonate heat shield to prevent the hot end from cooling down.

Plastic mulch must stay dry or be baked before extruding. Excessive moisture leads to bubbling and bubbles in the extruded filament. Contamination of the mulch also leads to poor filament quality.

Hub Mid-Drive Ebike

Designed and fabricated by Lawrence Chang

November 2017-July 2018

This bike was the continuation of my 3d printed electric bike.

I wanted to create a more permanent and better performing version of my 3d printed electric bike. I had purchased a used hub motor and decided to make it a mid drive because I could not find any hub motor rims, for any reasonable price, which suited by bike and I wanted to avoid lacing the hub motor to my rim directly.

The mount was machined from ¼ aluminum plate and some large aluminum U-channel. An adapter for the free wheel was turned and threaded on the lathe. The whole mount is bolted into the water bottle holder screws you find standard on mst bikes.

A second chainring an a crank arm free wheel was added to allow the pedals to idle while the hub motor ran.

Using NESE no solder packs and a bunch of recycled cells from previous projects. I created two 13s 4p battery packs housed inside harbor freight ammo cases. All in all, even with the used cells I could pull 25 miles of range from the two packs.

Desktop CNC Router CAD

Designed by Lawrence Chang

May 2018-July 2018

As I am a part of FRC robotics team #8, my team could greatly benefit from a cnc router capable of machining aluminum profiles for the robots that we build. Increasing our part output with the flexibility of multiple machines is ideal for an FRC robotics environment. I wanted to keep the entire project under 1.5k using generic ebay, and aliexpress parts. The router is still in the design phase and is based off of aluminum t-slot extrusion.

Micro Electric Motorbike

Designed and fabricated by Lawrence Chang

August 2018-December 2018

With this project, I wanted to create a highly transportable electric motorbike that could fit easily in the trunk of a car but still pack the power and speed of a full sized electric bike.

The Build

The entire micro bike frame is custom fabricated and features full suspension.

Features fully enclosed electronics, switch panel, and embedded charging port.

Head and rear lights are toggled using a steering tube embedded toggle switch.

Custom upholstered seat using an old pair of jeans for ruggedness!

Runs off an 1800w brushless motor and two 13s 4p 18650 packs in parallel.
